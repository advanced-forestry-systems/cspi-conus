## Caveats and considerations

1. **Federal partner sensitivity.** The finding that FIA SITECLCD has been tracking biomass growth rather than site index is empirically sound but rhetorically delicate. The framing in all three drafts is "the field has been quietly carrying biomass-growth structure for decades while being labeled as a height-growth measure" rather than "the FIA classification is wrong." This is the right register. Confirm with co-authors before public dissemination.

2. **Embargo timing.** The Zenodo deposit (v2.0.0) is already live and citable. The manuscript is in draft. The press release should not run before the manuscript is at least under review at FEM, to avoid the embarrassment of a heavily-promoted finding that fails peer review.

3. **Figure use.** Figure 8 (mean productivity by SITECLCD) and Figure 11 (decision tree) are the most legible to non-specialist audiences. These are the figures to lead with in news and social. Figure 7 (the 30 m CONUS map) is the most visually striking and is the right choice for press release headers.

4. **Cite ORCID and concept DOI.** All three drafts use 10.5281/zenodo.20515034 as the concept DOI. This always resolves to the latest version, so the references will not break when v2.0.1 or v2.1.0 release.
