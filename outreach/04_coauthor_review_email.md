**Title:** Beyond site index: FIA's own classification system disagrees with site index but agrees with a multi-dimensional composite of forest productivity at 30 m across the conterminous United States

**Headline finding:** A random forest predicting the FIA seven-class site productivity classification from biomass growth alone reaches OOB R² = 0.808; from site index alone, R² = 0.751. A six percentage point gap. The classification practitioners have used since the 1950s has been tracking biomass growth more closely than site index, and the data show forest productivity is empirically multi-dimensional across 66,000 plus FIA plots covering CONUS.

The release accompanying the paper is CSPI v2.0.0 at Zenodo concept DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20515034.
