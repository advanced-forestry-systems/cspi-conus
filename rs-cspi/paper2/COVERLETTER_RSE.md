To the Editors, Remote Sensing of Environment

I am submitting the manuscript "Beyond site index: a remote sensing composite site productivity index that resolves microsite variability across the conterminous United States, independent of forest inventory" for consideration as a research article.

For a century the operational measure of forest productivity has been site index, a height-based quantity estimated from sparse, coordinate-fuzzed inventory plots that cannot resolve the microsite variability productivity actually exhibits. This manuscript develops a different measure by inverting the usual remote sensing workflow. Rather than predicting an inventory quantity with remote sensing as a covariate, it uses satellite-measured productivity (MODIS net primary productivity, GEDI and NASA-CMS biomass) as the response and a wall-to-wall climate, terrain, soil, and canopy stack as the predictors, with no inventory data in the modeling chain. The fitted relationships are mapped across all forested cells at 1 km and downscaled to 30 m, then combined into a composite productivity index.

Three results may interest the journal's readership. First, the wall-to-wall, response-side design yields complete spatial coverage and, under spatially blocked validation, a genuine gain on structural targets. Second, the index validates against independent AmeriFlux tower productivity (r = 0.66) and diverges from height-based site index along a flux-versus-height axis confirmed in the inventory ground data itself and, independently, against British Columbia inventory ground samples. Third, a resolution analysis identifies 30 m as the sweet spot for terrain-driven microsite productivity. The work is deliberately conservative about its limits: the index is a site and standing-productivity descriptor rather than a growth-rate predictor, and the manuscript states where it does and does not validate.

All trained models, surfaces, and analysis code are openly deposited at Zenodo (concept DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20827436) and on GitHub, so the work is fully reproducible. The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and has a single author with no competing interests.

Thank you for considering it.
